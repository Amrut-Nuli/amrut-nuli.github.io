Cotton Picking Robot
Precision Agriculture
Academic Project  ·  Design & Fabrication

Design and build an efficient, cost-effective semi-automatic cotton-picking robot to address challenges of manual cotton harvesting — highly labour-intensive and limiting farmer profitability.

SolidWorks Scissor Mechanism & Robotic Arm Calculations ANSYS Python / YOLO V8 Arduino IDE TinkerCAD Ultimaker CURA
  • Drive Sizing: 4WD gear-motor configuration; each wheel requires 0.288 N·m torque against field terrain rolling resistance and 5° grade — met by 10 RPM DC gear motors.
  • Machine Vision: 16 MP camera paired with YOLO V8 algorithm for real-time cotton boll detection.
  • Adaptive Height: 4-stage linkage delivering 207–773 mm lift stroke (566 mm range); lead-screw actuated at 56.6 N design force with FOS 1.5.
  • Power & Capacity: Full system draw of 38.5 W at 12V; peak stall current of 14.95 A — sized for 12V 10 Ah LiPo with 15C discharge rating.2,583 cm³ storage volume.
  • Remote Operation: Semi-autonomous control via custom IoT mobile app with WiFi operational range.
  • Weight Optimization: Lightweight 3D-printed PLA robotic arm and gripper to minimise energy consumption.

Dynamic Analysis of Automotive Front Transmission Axle
ICAPSM 2024
7th Sem Project  ·  Presented at ICAPSM 2024

Comprehensive FEA and rotordynamic study of a front transmission shaft axle to identify critical speeds, assess vibrational mode stability, and enhance dynamic response by integrating hybrid bearing technologies.

SolidWorks ANSYS Workbench Modal Analysis Campbell Diagram Bearing Calculations Rotordynamics
  • Hybrid Bearings: Explored replacement of conventional angular contact ball bearings with non-contact magnetic bearings to reduce friction, energy loss, and wear.
  • FEA & Modal Analysis: SolidWorks 3D modelling + ANSYS Workbench static structural, modal analysis, and Campbell diagram stability analysis.
  • Field Validation: Integrated field survey data from authorized service centers to address real-world bearing seal degradation and contaminant ingress failures.
  • MAM Configuration: Magnetic-Angular-Magnetic hybrid identified as optimal — 18.38% reduction in peak mode deformation vs. conventional AAA baseline.
  • Frequency Optimization: Operating frequencies lowered by 4.59%, resulting in improved transmission efficiency.

Jig & Fixture Design for Precision Drilling
Manufacturing
Academic Project  ·  Process Planning & GD&T

Design and develop a process plan for a jig facilitating the drilling of three Ø7 mm holes on a shaft component, solving the challenge of precision hole location on cylindrical surfaces.

SolidWorks GD&T Process Planning Material Selection 2D Drafting
  • Functional Efficiency: 3-2-1 principle constrains all degrees of freedom — repeatable drilling within ±0.2 mm positional tolerance.
  • Quality Assurance: Hardened OHNS jig bushes and rubber-lined lid protect N5–N7 surface finish while ensuring H7 hole accuracy.
  • Error Prevention: Fool-proofing pins prevent incorrect shaft loading — critical for high-volume manufacturing.
  • Standard Compliance: Adheres to ISO limits, fits, and tolerances standards throughout.

Autonomous Multi-Functional Cleaning Robot
Mechatronics
Academic Project  ·  Design & Fabrication

Design and fabricate a cost-effective autonomous mechatronic floor-cleaning robot capable of simultaneous dry vacuuming and wet mopping using Arduino-based control and ultrasonic obstacle avoidance.

SolidWorks Arduino IDE TinkerCAD C++ Bluetooth IoT Motor Sizing
  • Drive System: Omni-directional, differential-drive platform driven by custom C++ obstacle-avoidance algorithms.
  • Actuation: Four 60 RPM DC motors from rigorous torque and incline calculations — 1.5 N·m per wheel.
  • Cleaning System: Localised pneumatic suction combined with 12V submersible pump for dual-action cleaning.
  • Result: 15W power consumption profile; prototype manufacturing cost reduced by over 90% vs. commercial equivalents.
